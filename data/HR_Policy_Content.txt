SECTION 1: PAID TIME OFF (PTO) POLICY

1.1 Eligibility
All full-time employees who have completed a minimum of 90 calendar days of continuous employment are eligible for PTO benefits. Part-time employees working 20 or more hours per week accrue PTO on a pro-rata basis.

1.2 Annual PTO Allocation
- Junior Employees (Level 1–3): 20 days per calendar year
- Senior Employees (Level 4–6): 25 days per calendar year
- Directors and above (Level 7+): 30 days per calendar year

1.3 Accrual and Carryover
PTO accrues on a bi-weekly basis. Unused PTO may be carried over to the next calendar year, up to a maximum of 10 days. Any unused PTO beyond the carryover limit will be forfeited on December 31st. Employees will receive automated notifications at 30 and 60 days before year-end.

1.4 PTO Request Process
Employees must submit PTO requests through the HR Portal a minimum of 5 business days in advance. Requests exceeding 5 consecutive days require manager and HR Director approval. Emergency PTO may be granted retroactively with a valid justification submitted within 48 hours.

SECTION 2: COMPANY HOLIDAYS

2.1 Designated Company Holidays for 2026
The following days are recognized as paid company holidays:
- New Year's Day: January 1
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day: January 19
- Presidents' Day: February 16
- Memorial Day: May 25
- Independence Day: July 4 (observed July 3 if on Saturday)
- Labor Day: September 7
- Thanksgiving: November 26–27 (Thursday & Friday)
- Winter Break: December 24–26

2.2 Floating Holidays
Each employee receives 2 floating holidays per calendar year, to be used at their discretion. Floating holidays do not carry over and must be used by December 31.

SECTION 3: LEAVE OF ABSENCE

3.1 Medical Leave
Employees are entitled to up to 12 weeks of unpaid medical leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA). Short-term disability coverage begins after 14 consecutive calendar days of absence and covers 60% of base salary for up to 26 weeks.

3.2 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 6 weeks of fully paid leave. Parental leave must commence within 12 months of the birth or adoption date.

3.3 Bereavement Leave
Up to 5 paid days for immediate family members (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Up to 3 paid days for extended family members (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle).

SECTION 4: EMPLOYEE CONDUCT & COMPLIANCE

4.1 Code of Conduct
All employees must adhere to the company's Code of Conduct, which prohibits discrimination, harassment, and retaliation. Violations must be reported through the anonymous Ethics Hotline or directly to the HR department.

4.2 Remote Work Policy
Eligible employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Remote work arrangements must be documented in the HR system. Employees must maintain a dedicated workspace and be available during core business hours (10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time).

4.3 Performance Reviews
Annual performance reviews are conducted in Q4 (October–December). Mid-year check-ins occur in Q2 (April–June). Performance ratings directly influence annual bonus calculations and promotion eligibility.

SECTION 5: COMPENSATION & BENEFITS

5.1 Salary Structure
Salaries are benchmarked annually against industry standards using data from Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and internal compensation studies. Adjustments are effective April 1 of each year.

5.2 Annual Bonus
Eligible employees may receive an annual performance bonus ranging from 5% to 20% of base salary, based on individual and company performance. Bonuses are paid in February following the performance year.

5.3 Health & Wellness Benefits
- Medical: PPO and HMO options with the company covering 85% of premiums
- Dental: 100% company-paid for employee; 50% for dependents
- Vision: 100% company-paid for employee
- Mental Health: 12 free therapy sessions per year through the Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Wellness Stipend: $500 annual reimbursement for gym, fitness apps, or wellness activities
